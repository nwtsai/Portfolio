About Me

My name is Nathan Tsai, and I am a motivated individual with a passion for programming. I am currently a sophomore studying Computer Science and Engineering at UCLA.

Discovering passions is my passion. In order to do this, I constantly expose myself to both the front end and back end aspects of programming. For example, as a result of constructing and designing this website, I discovered a keen interest in web development.

Aside from programming, I enjoy volunteering, hiking, and playing the piano, as well as getting involved in on-campus clubs and organizations. I became a member of Theta Tau, the nation's oldest and largest Professional Engineering Fraternity. I also expanded my horizons and joined Foundations Choreography, where I performed on stage and discovered a passion for hip-hop dancing.

Skills

Throughout the years, I've picked up a variety of programming languages. I've developed a deep understanding of C++ and Java. I've also dabbled in C and Javascript, and completed an online Python course via Coursera. In addition to these programming languages, I am also well-versed in markup languages such as HTML and CSS.

I am also familiar with a multitude of object-oriented programming concepts, such as abstraction, encapsulation, and polymorphism as a result of inheritance; furthermore, I have considerable experience with applications of data structures, efficiencies of sorting algorithms, and the Big O notation of nested loops and algorithms.

In order to apply these programming concepts, I utilize IDE's such as Microsoft Visual Studio, XCode, and Eclipse. I also have considerable experience with Salesforce, a cloud-based customer relationship management software, as well as Solidworks, a computer-aided design platform.
